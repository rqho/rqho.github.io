This blog now supports LaTeX math expressions powered by KaTeX! Here are some examples:

## Inline Math

You can write inline math like this: $E = mc^2$ or $\pi \approx 3.14159$.

The quadratic formula is $x = \dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$.

## Display Math

For larger expressions, use display math blocks:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

The Fourier transform:

$$
\mathcal{F}(f)(k) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) e^{-2\pi i k x} dx
$$

## Matrix Example

You can also render matrices:

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
x \\
y
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
ax + by \\
cx + dy
\end{pmatrix}
$$

## Complex Expressions

Even complex mathematical expressions work beautifully:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}
$$

$$
\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1
$$

That's it! Your blog now supports beautiful LaTeX math rendering.
